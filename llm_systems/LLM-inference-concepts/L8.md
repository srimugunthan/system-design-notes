# L8: MoE & Model Parallelism

## Introduction

Mixture of Experts (MoE) and model parallelism enable scaling LLMs beyond single-device limits. As inference engineers, we use these techniques to serve massive models efficiently. This section covers MoE routing, load balancing, and various parallelism strategies.

## Mixture of Experts (MoE)

MoE replaces dense FFN layers with sparse expert layers.

### Theoretical Background

Instead of all parameters active per token, route tokens to subset of experts.

Capacity: number of tokens per expert per batch.

### Expert Routing Strategies

#### Top-K Routing

Route each token to top-k experts by gating scores.

#### Theoretical Background

Gating network: g(x) = softmax(W_g x)

Route to experts with highest g_i

#### Practical Implementation

Load balancing: auxiliary loss to balance expert usage.

Reference:
- Paper: Shazeer, N., et al. (2017). Outrageously Large Neural Networks: The Sparsely-Gated Mixture-of-Experts Layer. arXiv:1701.06538

### Load Balancing

Prevent expert overload.

#### Theoretical Background

Capacity factor: multiplier for expected load.

Auxiliary loss: encourage uniform expert utilization.

#### Practical Implementation

In Switch Transformer, capacity = (tokens_per_batch * capacity_factor) / num_experts

Reference:
- Paper: Fedus, W., et al. (2022). Switch Transformers: Scaling to Trillion Parameter Models with Simple and Efficient Sparsity. arXiv:2101.03961

## Model Parallelism

### Tensor Parallelism

Split model parameters across devices.

#### Theoretical Background

For linear layers: W = [W1, W2, ..., Wn] across GPUs.

AllReduce for gradients/outputs.

#### Practical Implementation

In Megatron-LM, used for training/inference.

Reference:
- Paper: Shoeybi, M., et al. (2019). Megatron-LM: Training Multi-Billion Parameter Language Models Using Model Parallelism. arXiv:1909.08053

### Pipeline Parallelism

Split model into stages across devices.

#### Theoretical Background

Stage 1: layers 1-k on GPU1

Stage 2: layers k+1-2k on GPU2

Micro-batching for pipelining.

#### Practical Implementation

GPipe: efficient pipeline parallelism.

Reference:
- Paper: Huang, Y., et al. (2019). GPipe: Efficient Training of Giant Neural Networks using Pipeline Parallelism. arXiv:1811.06965

### Expert Parallelism

Specialized for MoE: experts on different devices.

#### Theoretical Background

Route tokens to devices hosting required experts.

All-to-all communication for expert outputs.

#### Practical Implementation

In DeepSpeed-MoE, combines with tensor parallelism.

Reference:
- Paper: Rajbhandari, S., et al. (2022). DeepSpeed-MoE: Advancing Mixture-of-Experts Inference and Training to Power Next-Generation AI Scale. arXiv:2201.05596

## Conclusion

MoE and model parallelism enable efficient scaling of LLMs. Routing strategies and load balancing are critical for MoE performance, while parallelism techniques distribute computation across devices. In production, these are combined with inference optimizations for massive model serving.
